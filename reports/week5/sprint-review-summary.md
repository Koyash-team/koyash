## Sprint Goal reviewed

MVP v2 — make the budget expectation honest, add a skin-type mini-quiz for users who don't
know their type, integrate LLM-generated justifications, and apply the customer's design
and copy feedback; in parallel, document the product architecture (static/dynamic/deployment
views + ADRs) and the development process.

## Delivered increment demonstrated

- **Honest budget presentation** (deployed): each budget option is a tier with an
  approximate per-product price and an approximate whole-set total; the results screen shows
  the real total plus an "approximate price — check the link" note.
- **LLM justifications** (deployed): a warm, human "why" under each product, generated by
  gpt-4o-mini over the already-selected products (justification-only).
- **Design & copy** (Figma): rewritten landing (dedup blocks, no gradient), the skin-type
  mini-quiz screens, and the trimmed short questionnaire — shown as the agreed direction.
- **Skin-type mini-quiz** (Figma): "I don't know" opens a 4-question mini-quiz with 5
  possible results that flows back into the skin-type question.
- **Architecture & process documentation**: the maintained views, ADRs, and development
  process were noted as delivered.

## UAT results (customer-executed, same recorded session)

- **UAT-004 — honest budget presentation:** Passed. The customer reviewed the live budget
  options and the real total and approved them ("it's great", "well done"); the clear range
  distribution made the price easier to understand.
- **UAT-005 — LLM justification:** Passed at this stage. The customer read the live
  justifications and accepted them for now, noting a few minor tone points to refine later
  and that the model may later be switched to Gemini (with a re-adapted prompt).
- Old scenarios UAT-001/002/003 remain valid and unchanged.

## Feedback and requested changes

- **Landing copy:** reword "процесс подбора" → "процесс опроса"; the customer will send a
  clearer reference wording for the left "why trust us" column; the right column is good.
- **Contacts / "hat":** remove the abrupt contacts block ("hat") and place the contacts in
  the bottom bar so it reads as a casual footer aligned with the landing.
- **Gradient:** confirmed not needed — the imagery already provides the colour.
- **Short questionnaire:** keep the explanations (they carry the brand tone), but trim the
  text on 2–3 slides and send the customer both versions to compare.
- **Brand voice:** speak on behalf of the brand — use "koyash" rather than "sun", and the
  brand voice is masculine (e.g. "проанализировал").
- **Skin-type mini-quiz:** approved ("brilliant, confirmed") — text and design.

## Approvals

- Landing direction — approved with the small copy comments above.
- Skin-type mini-quiz — approved.
- Honest budget presentation — approved.
- LLM justifications — accepted for this stage.
- Special-condition handling by our own deterministic rules — approved (see below).

## Product decisions

- **Special conditions (pregnancy / dermatitis / rosacea):** the customer confirmed we may
  filter these ourselves with deterministic rules (e.g. exclude retinoids for pregnancy);
  she may share an ingredient guide, but it's optional. Not an LLM task.
- **Age / experience:** collected for statistics only — they do not affect the
  recommendation.
- **Prices:** the "average price" approach and the honest budget presentation are approved;
  the customer confirmed live price scraping (e.g. Gold Apple) is impractical, matching our
  decision to show approximate prices with a link.

## Risks

- LLM tone still has minor residuals (occasional over-generic phrasing / rare
  mis-attribution); a model change to Gemini is anticipated, which will need prompt re-work.
- The special-condition ingredient mapping is best-effort (not medical advice) and may need
  refinement from the customer's guide.
